Welcome to Our Exhibition

Each piece in this collection explores the complex history of cannabis prohibition and its disproportionate impact on communities of color. This project was developed as part of Cannabis 170 with Dr. Poli.

"The criminalization of cannabis has never been about the plant itself, but rather about who was using it." — Cultural historian

1930

Regulatory roots grown form racist, hidden Meaning

The 1930s marked the beginning of a new era in cannabis policy, with the rise of anti-cannabis sentiment fueled by racial prejudice and misinformation.

The Marihuana Tax Act of 1937 effectively criminalized cannabis, leading to widespread arrests and stigmatization of users, particularly among marginalized communities.

As the decades progress, focus on the longstanding effects of the policy implemented during this transformational time.

Policy and Figurers to Consider

  • Marihuana tax act: Passed in 1937 by Henry Anslinger. Presented goal was to regulate the "evil" cannabis plant, first time word marihuana used in policy
  • Henry Anslinger: Ironically, the father of cannabis regulation in the United States. Openly expressed racist and Xenophobic attitudes, starts the trend of using cannabis as a vehichle for ulterior motifs
