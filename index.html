Cannabis Justice: Confronting Racial Discrimination Through Art

Examining the intersection of race, policy, the prison industrial complex and cannabis prohibition

Introduction Decade Questions

Welcome to Our Exhibition

This exhibit examines how cannabis prohibition has disproportionately affected communities of color through policy, media portrayal, and criminal justice over the decades. Please keep an open mind and an open heart as you examine the Art Piece

As You make your way through this section of the exhibit, please take some time to read the questions below, and think critically about your answers to each

"The criminalization of cannabis has never been about the plant itself, but rather about who was using it."

Questions Through the Decades

1930s

  • Who was Henry Anslinger, and how did he use media to influence cannabis policy?
  • What role did racism and xenophobia play in the criminalization of cannabis during this era?
  • How were early cannabis policies used to control marginalized communities?

1940s

  • What political or social movements influenced drug policy in the 1940s?
  • How did wartime propaganda shift the narrative around cannabis use?
  • Was there a noticeable shift in enforcement patterns in this decade?

1950s

  • How did the Cold War and McCarthyism influence cannabis policy?
  • What role did law enforcement play in reinforcing cannabis-related fear?
  • What were the racial implications of drug arrests during this period?

1960s

  • How did the counterculture and civil rights movements intersect with cannabis use?
  • What kind of surveillance or targeting did these groups face?
  • How was cannabis used as a political tool in suppressing dissent?
